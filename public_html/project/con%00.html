Anime/Manga Conventions

Conventions are gatherings of fans of anime/manga. These gatherings are hosted annually at convention centers, hotels, college campuses and usually lasting multiple days (usually over the weekend) they feature various activities, panels (discussions), contests, and fan-made products for sale. Bigger conventions can also feature industry artists, publishers, and studios presenting their work to their audience.

Prevalent at conventions is Cosplay. The word comes from costume play and is when cosplayers wear costumes of a character - usually from anime/manga, a game, a TV show, or some other media with a visual element. Though cosplayers may also cosplay a character of their own design.

Cosplayers often network, creating a subculture, particularly at conventions where they wear the result of their labor and where there are often cosplay competitions.

Of course there are the Internet memes Rule 63 and Rule 34 of the Internet. Rule 63 states that any given character will be depicted with their gender switched. And Rule 34 states that there will be a skimpy version.
