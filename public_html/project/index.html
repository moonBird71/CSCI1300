A Peek into Online Anime/Manga Geekdom

This website is made with the purpose of informing a general audience about the online subculture of self-identified anime/manga geeks.
Most geek sites are created by those already in that subculture as a forum for others like them and assume that visitors to the site are already part of their subculture and know what to expect. Or can figure it out after sticking around after a while.
Not this one - this website has explanations aimed at people who aren't already a part of this field of interest.



The Online Subculture

Keep in mind that we are talking about the Internet - new ideas, memes, and fanspeak terms come up all the time.
And this is meant to be Anime/Manga 101: a very general overview. So this is not completely up to date nor does it go into particulars.





Geek, not Nerd

Language changes over time and terms mean something a little different every few years. The current meaning of "geek" and "nerd" by self-identified geeks and nerds are:
A "geek" is someone who is fascinated by a very specific field. This field can be computers, games, art, a TV series, anything.
A "nerd" is someone who is highly intelligent and tends towards introversion.





General Fanspeak

This is just a list of terms that pertain to the subject as a whole. There are similar lists that pertain to particular areas to be found in their respective pages


  • Genres: (other than generic genres like romance, action, etc.)
  • Crack/Parody: funny, breaks suspension of disbelief
  • Mecha: will feature giant, often humanoid, robots
  • Kodomomuke: aimed at children. Like the Japanese version of Sesame Street
  • Shonen: aimed at pre-teen to teen boys. Lots of action
  • Shoujo: aimed at pre-teen to teen girls. Usually has romance
  • Seinen: aimed at young men. Action, but more mature. More psychological and flirty
  • Josei: aimed at young women. Romance/relationships but more realistic and can be darker
  • Shounen ai (male)/shoujo ai (female): same-sex romance
  • Yaoi/yuri: same-sex romance, more mature
  • Ecchi: perverted, naughty but done playfully (usually)
  • Hentai: overtly sexual
  • Literary devices:
  • Shipping/pairings: when observers want to see a romantic relationship between a specific pair
  • Fanservice: scenes to excite/tease viewers. Usually involves attractive characters in skimpy clothing, suffering wardrobe malfunctions/clothing damage, or undressing.
  • Canon: official storyline
  • Headcanon: ideas accepted among fans as a sort of personal canon that was not actually in the original work
  • Fanon: ideas common among fanfiction authors
  • Word of God (WoG): explicitly stated by the author
  • Fanworks:
  • Fanart
  • Doujinshi
  • Fanfiction
  • Cosplay
